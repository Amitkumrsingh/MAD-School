NIFT ENTRANCE EXAM

Understanding the ins and outs…

The National Institute of Fashion Technology, also known as NIFT, is a premium institute in India for fashion education established in 1986. NIFT Institute was set up under the guidance of the Ministry of Textiles, Govt of India (A statutory institute governed by the NIFT Act 2006). NIFT has its presence all across India with 18 campuses in major cities including Chennai, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Kolkata, Mumbai, and many more.

NIFT offers 5 programs, of which 2 are undergraduate programs and 3 are master programs.

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS


  • Bachelor of Design (B.Des), 4 years
  • Bachelor of Fashion Technology (B.Ftech), 4 years

MASTER PROGRAMS


  • Master of Design (M.Des), 2 years
  • Master of Fashion Technology (M.Ftech), 2 years
  • Master of Fashion Management (MFM), 2 years

To get into one of these programs students have to take up the NIFT Entrance Exam.



NIFT Campus Locations

Each NIFT campus has its own strengths and specializations. For example, the NIFT campus in New Delhi is known for its strong program in fashion design, while the NIFT campus in Bangalore is known for its strong program in textile design. You do not have to worry about this as we will guide you and help you consider the NIFT college that you need to choose based on the specialization that you want to pursue.

WHY IS COACHING IMPORTANT FOR NIFT ENTRANCE EXAM?

Get to know why…

When it comes to the NIFT entrance exam, there are students who clear the exam without coaching classes. But why do we say that coaching classes are important? Here are some of the reasons why students require coaching for the NIFT entrance exam.


Preparing halfway before the NIFT Exam:



Many students start their preparation for the NIFT exam 6 months or 3 months before the exam dates are announced. Usually, students start to panic and do not follow a proper flow while preparing. This is one of the reasons why our MAD School Hyderabad coaching institute has 3 months and 6 month coaching classes in both online and offline mode to help you prepare for the NIFT entrance exam.

Getting started with the preparation:



Many students do not know how to start and proceed with the preparation for NIFT entrance exams. This is why our faculties in MAD School NIFT coaching class prepare and implement effective strategies personalized to your needs to get started with the NIFT coaching.

Learning approach:



The learning approach particularly followed for NIFT entrance exam is different when compared to other all India entrance examinations. When it comes to NIFT entrance examinations you are tested for these skills - aptitude, sketching, model making, etc. This is one of the reasons why you require a coaching class for NIFT exam preparations.

Maintaining balance between regular class and entrance exam preparations:



Most of the students start preparing for the exams after they complete their 10th boards. It becomes a challenge to maintain a balance between the regular school classes and the entrance exam preparations. This is why coaching classes and institutes help in guiding you and test your skills with NIFT practice mock tests and section-wise tests.

1. What is CAT and GAT in NIFT?

Creative Ability Test (CAT) and General Ability Test (GAT) are two components of the NIFT entrance exam. These tests are Computer Based Test (CBT).

CAT: It is a 2-hour long exam that tests your creativity, problem-solving skills, and visualization abilities.

GAT: It is a 2-hour long exam that tests your quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, and language skills.

The total number of questions asked in these sections varies from 120 to 150 and depends on the program that you select in NIFT. Both sections have negative markings. The cut-off score for this section varies from year to year, based on the difficulty of the exam.

Mad School provides coaching for CAT and GAT in an effective way that will help you clear the exam and obtain seats in NIFT.

2. Are NIFT placements good in Hyderabad campus?

Yes, the NIFT placements in Hyderabad are good. The campus has a strong alumni network, and many companies like IKEA, Bewakoof.com, Woodland, and many more come to recruit students from NIFT Hyderabad. The average salary for NIFT graduates from Hyderabad is around INR 10-13 lakhs per annum.

These companies offer various positions to students in their company related to design. Getting into one of these companies can be challenging, but if you are a good student with a strong portfolio you can easily get into one of these companies.

The eligibility for the NIFT entrance exam varies for different programs that you apply for. To get admission into NIFT you need to pass the NIFT entrance exam.

  • Cleared the NIFT entrance exam.

Bachelor of Fashion Technology (B.Ftech)

4. How to prepare for the NIFT entrance exam?

Follow these steps to start preparing for NIFT entrance exam:

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus, which includes the Creativity Aptitude Test (CAT) and General Aptitude Test (GAT).
  • Join a NIFT coaching class to effectively manage your time and receive expert guidance.
  • Obtain recommended NIFT preparation books (Mad school coaching provides books as well) to cover all aspects of the exam.
  • Solve previous year's question papers to become familiar with the exam format and identify your strengths and weaknesses.
  • Take mock tests to simulate the exam environment and practice under timed conditions.
  • Stay motivated throughout your preparation and remember your goal of studying at NIFT.

5. What are the scholarships offered at NIFT Hyderabad?

These are the scholarships that are offered in the NIFT Hyderabad Campus:

“SARTHAK” NIFT FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE SCHEME” - This scheme provides financial assistance to students who are from economically weaker backgrounds, but have good academic merit.

“UDAAN” - This is a means-cum-merit scheme for foreign studies. This scheme provides financial assistance to students who are from an economically weaker background and have good academic merit and wish to continue their studies abroad.

At our coaching center, there are faculties who will help you to apply for these scholarships if you qualify for the same.

6. How to make a fashion portfolio?

Mad School - NIFT Coaching center will help you create fashion portfolios with the best quality and presentation.

  • To create a fashion portfolio, gather your best fashion-related work, organize it, and choose a format (digital or physical).
  • Start with a compelling title page, add brief descriptions to contextualize each piece, and showcase a diverse range of styles, materials, and techniques.
  • Highlight any relevant technical skills and include process work, such as sketches and mood boards.
  • Keep updating the portfolio according to your audience and consider creating an online version for wider reach.
