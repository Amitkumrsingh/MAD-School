Candidates who have cleared the NID DAT Prelims for the masters of design in NID have to appear for NID Studio Test, also called as NID DAT Mains. This is the second round of the examination where the students are evaluated for their prompt response to situations, and their ability to think creatively.

The selection process for master of design has 4 parts which includes submitting an SoP, design portfolio, interview, and studio test.

Some of the topics or activities that are expected in Studio Test are doodling, sketching or drawing, hands-on model making, material manipulation, and more.

The NID DAT mains exam has more weightage than the NID DAT prelims exam. Prelims has a weightage of 30% and Mains has a weightage of 70%. At MAD School, we provide coaching for all the parts of the mains exam such as the SoP, interview, design portfolio, and studio test.

FAQs


1. What is NID DAT Exam?

A: NID DAT stands for National Institute of Design - Design Aptitude Test. This exam is conducted for the candidates to aspire to study at NID. The exam is conducted in two phases for B.des and M.des students as NID Prelims and NID Mains.

2. Is there an age limit to apply for NID DAT exam?

A: The maximum age for B.Des candidates is 20 years, with a 3-year relaxation for reserved category candidates. For M.Des, the upper age limit is 30 years.

3. How many times can I attempt the NID Mains exam?

A: There is no limit in attempting the NID DAT exams.

4. Is portfolio required for NID?

A: Yes, a portfolio is required for NID. If you are applying for M.Des, it is mandatory that you submit your design portfolio online or during the interview in the second round of exam.

5. How to prepare for NID DAT?

A: To clear the NID DAT exam you should focus on sketching, creativity, and problem-solving. Practice with past papers, enhance your observation skills, and stay updated on design trends. Build a strong portfolio showcasing your best work. Don't forget to manage your time during the exam.

6. Is NID tough?

A: NID is rated as a moderately difficult exam. However, it is easy to clear the exam if you are creative and have good problem-solving skills.

7. How many NID are there in India?

NID has a total of 7 campuses across India. The locations are listed below.

  • NID Ahmedabad
  • NID Gandhinagar
  • NID Bengaluru
  • NID Haryana
  • NID Madhya Pradesh
  • NID Andhra Pradesh
  • NID Assam
