UCEED Entrance Exam

UCEED is the abbreviation for Undergraduate Common Entrance Exam for Design. It assesses a student's aptitude for design, drawing skills, and creative thinking. MAD School's UCEED coaching program covers all the essential topics and provides students with the necessary skills and techniques to crack the exam and secure admission to top design schools.

The UCEED Entrance Examination is conducted for a duration of 3 hours. The UCEED Exam is conducted both as a computer-based test and pen and paper test.


UCCED Exam Syllabus 2023

In the UCEED examination question paper, there are two parts: Part A and Part B.

Part A:

  • Part A of the question paper tests the Drawing, Design, Creativity, and Logical Reasoning ability of the students.

Topics that are given below are usually asked in the examination.

  • Visualization and spatial ability
  • Observation and design sensitivity
  • Environmental and social awareness
  • Analytical and logical reasoning
  • Language and creativity
  • Design thinking and problem-solving

For more details on the syllabus visit the official website:
https://www.uceed.iitb.ac.in/2023/syllabus.html

Note this about the Part A of the UCEED Exam:

  • The duration to complete the Part A of the paper is 2.5 hours of 3 hours.
  • Part A of the question paper is again divided into 3 sections. Look at the chart below to understand better.

Part A

NAT MSQ MCQ
  • Numerical Answer Type
  • These questions have no options and are supposed to be entered as a real number with decimal digits up to two places.
  • Total Marks: 72
  • No Negative Marking
  • Multiple Select Questions
  • You have to select one or more options as the right answer from the four given options.
  • Total Marks: 72
  • Partial Marking
  • Multiple Select Questions
  • Out of the four given options one right answer needs to be selected.
  • Total Marks: 96
  • Negative Marking

Marking for Part A:

Section Type of Questions Number of Questions Marks for Correct Answer Marks for Wrong Answer Marks for Each Question Not Attempted Total Marks
1 NAT 18 4 0 0 72
2 MSQ 18 Partial Marking -1 0 72
3 MCQ 32 3 -0.71 0 96
Total 68 240

Part B:

  • Part B of the UCEED Examination consists of 1 drawing question.
  • Drawing: Ability to draw products, people or scenes in proportion with good line quality, composition, proportion, perspective, and shading.
  • Design aptitude: Capability to practically and appropriately respond to problems/situations with ingenuity and empathy.
  • It consists of 60 marks in total.
  • There is no negative marking for this section.
  • Part B of the UCEED examination is a pen and paper test.
  • The invigilator will provide an answer book where you have to give your answers.

UCEED Marks - Part A + Part B = 240 + 60 = 300 marks.

UCEED Exam Eligibility

  • Age:
    • Candidates must be born on or after October 1, 1998, if from the OPEN/EWS/OBC-NCL category,
    • and on or after October 1, 1993, if from the SC, ST, or PwD category.
  • Number of Attempts:
    • Candidates can attempt UCEED a maximum of two times, and only in consecutive years.
  • Qualifying Examination:
    • Candidates must have passed all subjects in their Class XII or equivalent examination in 2022,
    • or be appearing for the first time in 2023. Students from all streams are eligible.

Note: Candidates who appeared for the first time in their Class XII examination in 2021 or earlier are not eligible to appear in UCEED 2023.

1. What are the benefits of joining a UCEED coaching institute?

  • Mad School UCEED coaching can provide you with structured guidance and support throughout your preparation process.
  • We have experienced faculty who can help you understand the UCEED syllabus and exam pattern in depth.

2. What are some tips for preparing for the UCEED exam?

Here are some tips for preparing for the UCEED exam:

  • Start your preparation early and make a study plan.
  • Familiarize yourself with the UCEED syllabus and exam pattern.
  • Practice solving previous year's question papers and sample papers.
  • Improve your drawing skills and your understanding of design principles.
  • Take mock tests to assess your performance and identify your areas of improvement.

3. What is the best time to join a UCEED coaching institute?

The best time to join a UCEED coaching institute depends on your individual circumstances. However, it is generally recommended to start your coaching at least 6 months before the UCEED exam. This will give you enough time to cover the entire syllabus and practice for the exam.

4. How do I make the most out of my UCEED coaching experience?

Here are some tips for making the most of your UCEED coaching experience:

  • Attend all classes and participate actively.
  • Ask questions and clear your doubts.
  • Practice regularly using the study materials and practice papers.
  • Take mock tests seriously and analyze your performance.
  • Use the feedback provided by your faculty to improve your performance.
  • Stay motivated and focused on your goal.

5. What should I do if I am feeling stressed out while preparing for the UCEED exam?

It is normal to feel stressed out while preparing for the UCEED exam. However, it is important to manage your stress levels in a healthy way. Here are some tips:

  • Take breaks regularly.
  • Get enough sleep.
  • Eat healthy foods.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Talk to a friend.
