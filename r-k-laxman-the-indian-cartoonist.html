R K Laxman: The Indian Cartoonist Who Defined Satirical Art

If you are stepping into the world of Indian cartoons, you have to meet the person behind it, RK Laxman. Rasipuram Krishnaswami Iyer Laxman (RK. Laxman) was a renowned Indian Cartoonist, Illustrator, and humourist. He was an artist who drew funny pictures and made people laugh. He is known for creating "The Common Man." This character appeared in the newspaper The Times of India, starting in 1951, and it continued for many years.

RK Laxman used "The Common Man" to share insights about everyday life in India. He used to notice the interesting and silly things that happen in our everyday lives, add humor to them, and make us laugh.

In this blog, we're going to learn about RK Laxman's life and his amazing work. Take that sip of your coffee and chill as we dive into the world of one of India's most loved cartoonists.

R K Laxman - Childhood, Parents, and Siblings

Rasipuram Krishnaswami Iyer Laxman was born in 1921 in Mysore to a Tamil Brahmin family. His father Krishnaswami Iyer was a headmaster in a school. RK Laxman was the youngest of the eight children. His brother R K Narayan was also a renowned Novelist. It is safe to say that creativity and storytelling ran in the family.


R K Laxman’s Journey as an Artist

RK Laxman's family encouraged his early interest in art, and it was clear from a young age that he had a special talent for drawing. He was fascinated by the pictures in the magazines like The Strand, Punch, and Bystander, even before he could read. The support he received from his family, especially his father's belief in his artistic abilities, had a major hand in shaping his future as a celebrated cartoonist and illustrator.

The first one to notice this talent of R K Laxman was his teacher. She saw him drawing a peepal leaf. Also, he was very much inspired by the work of a famous British cartoonist named Sir David Low.

After completing his schooling, he applied to the Sir JJ Institute of Applied Arts, to commence his journey to become an artist. But his application was rejected stating that his work was not good enough and he lacked the talent that is required to get into the university. Well, even famous cartoonist like R K Laxam was rejected once.

But he never gave up. He then got a bachelor’s degree in Arts from the University of Mysore. While he was studying, he kept working as a freelancer. He drew cartoons for a magazine called "Swarajya," which was a right-wing monthly magazine. He also created an animated character named "Narada."

Career as a Cartoonist

RK Laxman began his career as a cartoonist by drawing cartoons for newspapers and magazines like Rohan, Swarajya, and Blitz. He also illustrated his elder brother's stories in The Hindu and drew political cartoons for local papers and magazines.

His first full-time job was as a political cartoonist for "The Free Press Journal" in Mumbai. In 1951, he joined the "Times of India" in Mumbai, where he worked for more than fifty years

Laxman's most famous creation is the "Common Man" character, who is depicted as a regular person observing and commenting on the development of democracy in India. His cartoons often focus on issues related to corruption and feature a cast of recurring characters. In simple terms, he used his cartoons to comment on and criticize corruption in society and the political system, with the "Common Man" representing the common people who bear witness to these events.

Iconic Creations by R K Laxman

The Common Man is RK Laxman's iconic creation. The character in his cartoons symbolizes the everyday hopes, dreams, troubles, and quirks of regular people in India. This character acted as a voice for the common folk in Laxman's cartoons.

Malgudi: Malgudi is a make-believe village that appears in the novels written by RK Laxman's brother, RK Narayan. Laxman created a visual representation of this village, giving it a unique and charming appearance. This was then brought to the big screens as a popular Indian Television Series “Malgudi Days”.

Gattu: When asked to create a mascot for Asian Paints, Laxman, who was smoking at the time, saw a little boy with a paintbrush emerging from the smoke. This led to the birth of "Gattu," the lovable mascot for the company.

Air Deccan: Air Deccan used the "Common Man" theme in its marketing strategy to highlight affordable and accessible air travel. This character, inspired by Laxman's cartoons, helped convey the idea that flying was not just for the elite but for everyone.

Laxman's Novels: In addition to his illustrations and cartoons, RK Laxman wrote two novels, "The Hotel Riviera" in 1988 and "The Messenger" in 1993. He also penned an autobiography titled "The Tunnel of Time" in 1998, sharing his life experiences and insights.

Awards and Honours

Padma Bhushan (1973): RK Laxman was honored with the Padma Bhushan, a prestigious award from the Indian government in 1973 for his outstanding contributions.

Karnataka Rajyotsava Award (1983): In 1983, he received the Karnataka Rajyotsava Award, which recognized his significant role in the field of art and culture.

Honorary Doctorate from the University of Mysore (2004): The University of Mysore conferred an honorary doctorate on him in 2004, acknowledging his achievements.

Padma Vibhushan (2005): Laxman was granted the Padma Vibhushan, one of India's highest civilian honors, in 2005 for his exceptional work.

Lifetime Achievement Award for Journalism (2008): In 2008, he received a Lifetime Achievement Award for his remarkable contributions to the field of journalism.


Tributes and Statues:

Commemorative Postage Stamp: The Indian Postal Service paid tribute to RK Laxman by featuring his "Common Man" character on a postage stamp in 1988. This stamp was issued to mark the 150th anniversary of the Times of India.

Bronze Statue at Symbiosis Institute, Pune: A bronze statue of "The Common Man," standing at an impressive 8.2 feet, was erected at the Symbiosis Institute in Pune in front of the Vishwa Bhavan building.

Statue on Worli Seaface, Mumbai: In 2007, a statue of "The Common Man," sculpted by Suresh Sakpal, was installed along the sea face on Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan Road, Worli Seaface, Mumbai.

R. K. Laxman Museum Entrance Statue: At the entrance of the R. K. Laxman Museum in Pune, you'll find another statue dedicated to "The Common Man," serving as a tribute to the artist's iconic creation.

Chair Named After R. K. Laxman at Symbiosis International University: To honor his legacy, Symbiosis International University named a chair after RK Laxman, commemorating his significant impact on the world of art and journalism.

R K Laxman Museum:

The R. K. Laxman Museum in Pune is a special place that holds more than 35,000 drawings and illustrations created by R. K. Laxman. These artworks are displayed in various rooms, kind of like different sections in a big library.

One of these rooms is all about the life of Laxman himself. In this room, you can see rare pictures from his childhood, when he was a little boy. You can also find photos of his older brother, who was a very famous writer, R. K. Narayan.

So, the museum is like a treasure trove of Laxman's amazing art and a place where you can learn about his life and even see pictures from his early days and those of his famous brother.

Personal Life

RK Laxman was first married to a lady named Kumari Kamala, who was a dancer and an actress. They didn't have any children, and they got divorced in 1960. After that, Laxman married someone else with the same first name, Kamala. This Kamala was his niece and also an author who wrote books for children.

In September 2003, Laxman had a serious health problem. He had a stroke, which made one side of his body, the left side, stop working properly. Although he got better to some extent, he was left partially paralyzed.

Sadly, Laxman passed away on India's Republic Day in 2015 when he was 93 years old. He had been in the hospital for three days before that because he had a urinary tract infection and problems with his chest. These health issues caused multiple organs in his body to stop working, and he passed away.
