NIFT Entrance Exam

Understand the ins and outs...


The National Institute of Fashion Technology, or NIFT, is an esteemed institution for fashion studies in India that was founded in 1986. NIFT was set up under the guidance of the Ministry of Textiles, Government of India (a statutory institution controlled by the NIFT Act of 2006). Having 18 campuses spread across India, including Chennai, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Kolkata, Mumbai, and many others.

NIFT offers 5 programmes (2 undergraduate programmes and 3 master's programmes).

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS


  • Bachelor of Design (B.Des), 4 years
  • Bachelor of Fashion Technology (B.Ftech), 4 years

MASTER PROGRAMS


  • Master of Design (M.Des), 2 years
  • Master of Fashion Technology (M.Ftech), 2 years
  • Master of Fashion Management (MFM), 2 years

To get into one of these programs students have to take up the NIFT Entrance Exam.

Why is coaching important for the NIFT exam?

Here’s why!


There certainly are students who clear the exam without coaching classes. But why do we say that coaching classes are important? Here are some of the reasons why students need coaching for the NIFT entrance exam.

Incomplete preparation before the NIFT Exam:

Many students begin studying for the NIFT exam 3–6 months before the exam dates are announced. Typically, students start to panic and do not follow a systematic flow while preparing. This is one of the reasons why the MAD School Bangalore coaching institute offers a 3 and 6-month coaching programme in both online and offline modes to help you prepare for the NIFT entrance exam.

Getting started with the preparation:

Most students have no idea where to start and yet proceed with their preparation for NIFT exams. Our MAD School NIFT coaching class faculties prepare and implement effective strategies personalised to your needs to get you started with the NIFT coaching.

Learning approach:

The learning approach followed for the NIFT entrance exam is different when compared to other all-India entrance examinations. During the NIFT entrance exam, you are tested for the following skills:

  • Aptitude
  • Sketching
  • Model making

Specialised coaching is required for each of these skill sets, which is one of the reasons why coaching classes for the NIFT exam are a requirement.

Balance!: Maintain a healthy balance between regular class and test preparations.

Most of the students start preparing for the exams after they complete their 10th board. Maintaining a balance between normal school sessions and entrance exam preparations becomes difficult.

This is why coaching classes and institutes help guide you and test your skills with NIFT practice mock tests and section-wise tests.

1. How do I prepare for the NIFT entrance exam?

To begin preparing for the NIFT entrance test, follow these steps:

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus, which include:
    • Creativity Aptitude Test (CAT)
    • General Aptitude Test (GAT)
  • Join an NIFT coaching class to learn how to manage your time efficiently and receive professional advice.
  • Obtain the recommended NIFT preparation books (MAD School Coaching provides up-to-date books for the NIFT exams) to cover all aspects of the exam.
  • Solve previous year's question papers to familiarize yourself with the exam format and identify your strengths and weaknesses. (MAD School Coaching has an extensive library of previous year's question papers.)
  • Take mock tests to simulate the exam environment and practice under pressure (MAD School Coaching offers mock exams with feedback).
  • Stay motivated throughout your preparation and remember your goal of studying at NIFT.

2. What exactly are CAT and GAT in NIFT?

The NIFT entrance test has two components: the Creative Ability Test (CAT) and the General Ability Test (GAT). These tests are Computer-Based Tests (CBT).

  • CAT
    • It is a 2-hour-long exam that tests you on:
      • Creativity
      • Problem-solving skills
      • Visualisation abilities
  • GAT
    • It is a 2-hour-long exam that tests you on:
      • Quantitative aptitude
      • Logical reasoning
      • Language skills

The total number of questions asked in these sections varies from 120 to 150 and depends on the programme that you select in NIFT. Both sections have negative markings. The cut-off score for this section varies from year to year based on the difficulty of the exam.

MAD School provides effective coaching for CAT and GAT to help you clear the exam and secure your NIFT seats.

5. How do I create a fashion portfolio?

To create a fashion portfolio, gather your best fashion-related work, organise it, and choose a format (digital or physical).
Start with a compelling title page, add brief descriptions to contextualise each piece, and showcase a diverse range of styles, materials, and techniques.Highlight any relevant technical skills and include process work, such as sketches and mood boards.
Continue to update the portfolio based on your audience, and consider creating an online version for wider exposure.
Mad School's NIFT Coaching Centre will help you create fashion portfolios with the best quality and presentation.

Yes, the NIFT placements in Bangalore are very good. The campus has a strong alumni network, and many companies like IKEA, Bewakoof.com, Woodland, and many more come to recruit students from NIFT Bangalore. The average salary for NIFT graduates from Bangalore is around INR 10–13 lakhs per annum.
