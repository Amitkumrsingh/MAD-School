NID ENTRANCE EXAM

Understanding the ins and outs…

The National Institute of Design (NID) is a premier design school in India that was established in 1961. It is the first design school in India to be declared as an ‘Institution of National Importance’ by an Act in the Indian Parliament. NID is an autonomous institute under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. It offers design courses in specializations such as Industrial, Communication, Textile, and IT Integrated (Experiential) Design.

NID offers 2 programs, one undergraduate program and one master's program.

BACHELOR OF DESIGN: (4 Years)


B.Des is an undergraduate program offered by NID and has multiple specializations as listed below. The eligibility to join B.Des course in NID is to pass the higher secondary examination (10+2) in any stream.

  • Animation Film Design
  • Exhibition Design
  • Film and Video Communication
  • Graphic Design
  • Ceramic and Glass Design
  • Furniture and Interior Design
  • Product Design
  • Textile Design
  • Communication Design
  • Industrial Design
  • Textile and Apparel Design

MASTER OF DESIGN: (2.5 Years)


M.Des is a post-graduate program offered by NID and has multiple specializations as listed below. The eligibility to join the M.Des course in NID is to have a Bachelor's degree (3 or 4 years) in any relevant specialization.

  • Animation Film Design
  • Film and Video Communication
  • Graphic Design
  • Photography Design
  • Ceramic and Glass Design
  • Furniture and Interior Design
  • Product Design
  • Toy and Game Design
  • Transportation and Automobile Design
  • Universal Design
  • Digital Game Design
  • Information Design
  • Interaction Design
  • New Media Design
  • Design for Retail Experience
  • Strategic Design Management
  • Apparel Design
  • Lifestyle Accessory Design
  • Textile Design

To get into one of these programs students have to take up the NID Entrance Exam.

NID DAT PRELIMS & MAINS

What are these exams?

NID Prelims

  • This is the first round of the Design Aptitude Test (DAT). This test is conducted as a written test that consists of objective and subjective questions.
  • NID Prelims carry a total of 100 marks and are conducted for a duration of 3 hours.
  • Both B.Des and M.Des students have to go through this but the weightage for both differs in the respective examinations.

Mad School provides coaching for NID DAT B.Des and NID DAT M.Des preparation separately.


NID Mains

  • Students who have enrolled for B.Des have to attend the second round which is the Studio test. Studio test is conducted offline. The students will be given a practical assessment that needs to be completed in a given time.
  • Students who have enrolled for M.Des have to attend the personal interview round.

Mad School provides coaching for the Studio test and NID DAT exam separately. Along with this, we have mock interviews with tips to attend the personal interview.

Students who wish to attend only the NID Mains coaching are welcome.

FAQs

Find your answers…

1. Is NID a government institute?

Yes, NID is a government institute. It is an autonomous institution under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. NID was established in 1961 in Ahmedabad and has expanded to include campuses in Gandhinagar and Bengaluru.

The institute is funded by the government, but it functions as an autonomous body. This means that it has its own governing board and is responsible for its own academic and administrative policies. However, the government still plays a role in NID's oversight, and the institute is required to submit annual reports to the Ministry of Commerce and Industry.

The fee structure at NID is subsidized by the government, but students are still required to pay a tuition fee. The amount of the fee varies depending on the course of study and the student's financial status.

2. Is coaching necessary for appearing in NID entrance exam?

The NID entrance exam is a challenging and competitive exam. Coaching can be a helpful way to prepare the students who attend schools and prepare for the exam. Coaching can provide you with the tools and resources you need to succeed, including practice tests, mock interviews, and guidance from experienced instructors.

However, coaching is not necessary for everyone. Some students are able to pass the NID exam without any coaching. If you are considering coaching, it is important to do your research and find an institute that offers a program based on your needs.

You can check out Mad Schools Timetable to find out if you have a coaching program based on your requirement. Or you can enquire about the same.
