Graphic Designer

Art Director:

An art director is a visual creative professional. The art director takes care of the visuals for a variety of projects, including advertising campaigns, magazines, newspapers, books, websites, and product packaging. Art directors are responsible for developing the overall visual style and concept of a project, and for managing a team of designers to bring that concept to life.

Art directors typically work in close collaboration with copywriters and other creative professionals to develop and execute creative ideas. They also need to have a strong understanding of typography, layout, color theory, and other design principles.

Salary Range: ₹3.9 LPA - ₹18.0 LPA

Some top companies that hire for this role are,

  • Amazon
  • Microsoft
  • Adobe
  • Netflix
  • Disney
  • Warner Bros.
  • Sony Pictures
  • BBC

UI/UX Designer:

As a UI/UX designer professional, you will be responsible for the user interfaces and user experiences of digital products, such as websites, mobile apps, and software. They work to create interfaces that are both visually appealing and easy to use. UI/UX designers also conduct user research to understand the needs and wants of their users, and to ensure that their designs meet those needs.

Salary Range: ₹5.5 LPA - ₹15.0 LPA

Some top companies that hire for this role are,

  • Adobe
  • Netflix
  • Disney
  • Airbnb
  • Uber
  • Spotify
  • Slack

Freelance Designers are independent professionals who offer their creative services to a variety of clients or businesses. They have the flexibility to work on diverse projects, ranging from logo design to marketing materials like brochures, posters, web graphics, and more.

Freelancers often manage their schedules and work remotely, offering a blend of artistic skill, business acumen, and project management capabilities. Freelancing will give you the freedom to negotiate terms, deliver creative solutions tailored to each client's needs, and manage multiple projects simultaneously. This role demands not only artistic expertise but also excellent communication and client management skills to maintain successful and lasting relationships with clients.

Visual Arts

Painter/Illustrator

Salary Range: ₹3.5 LPA - ₹10.0 LPA

As a painter/illustrator, you will be creating images using a variety of mediums, such as paint, ink, and digital tools. They may work on a variety of projects, including book covers, magazine illustrations, advertising campaigns, and product packaging. Painters/illustrators need to have a strong understanding of color theory, composition, and perspective. They also need to be able to work to tight deadlines and be able to take feedback from clients. You also need to have good knowledge of Illustrator software such as Adobe Illustrator and more.

Some of the companies that hire for this role are,

  • Illumination Entertainment
  • Sony Pictures Animation
  • Warner Bros. Animation
  • Nickelodeon
  • Cartoon Network
  • Scholastic
  • HarperCollins

Sculptor

As a sculptor, you will be creating three-dimensional works of art from a variety of materials, such as clay, metal, stone, wood, and glass. Sculptors use a variety of techniques to create their work, including carving, modeling, casting, and welding. They may also use digital tools to design and create their sculptures.

Sculptors often work on a variety of projects, including public art installations, monuments, fountains, and sculptures for private collections. They may also work on commercial projects, such as designing sculptures for products or packaging.

Salary Range: ₹3.9 LPA - ₹18.0 LPA

Some of the companies that hire for this role are,

  • Universal Pictures
  • Paramount Pictures
  • Industrial Light & Magic
  • Lucasfilm
  • Weta Workshop
  • Pixar Animation Studios

Printmaker

A printmaker is a visual artist who creates prints using a variety of techniques, such as etching, lithography, screen printing, and relief printing. Printmakers use these techniques to create original works of art, as well as to reproduce works of art by other artists.

Printmakers typically work in a studio setting, where they have access to the specialized tools and equipment required for printmaking. They may also work in a collaborative setting, such as a print shop or art workshop.

Salary Range: ₹3.5 LPA - ₹11.0 LPA

Some of the companies that hire for this role are,

  • Tamarind Institute
  • Hand Pulled Prints
  • Serigrafica
  • Counter Editions
  • Flatbed Press
  • High Roller Editions
  • Idem Editions

Digital Arts

Animator

An animator is a visual artist who creates the illusion of motion by drawing or using computer software to create a series of images that are displayed rapidly. Animators work on a variety of projects, including movies, television shows, video games, commercials, and online content.

The job duties of an animator vary depending on the type of project they are working on, but they typically include:

  • Developing the characters and environments for a project
  • Creating storyboards and animatics to plan out the action of a scene
  • Drawing or using computer software to create individual frames of animation
  • Timing and editing the animation to create a smooth and fluid sequence
  • Working with other animators, directors, and producers to ensure that the animation meets the creative vision of the project

Salary Range: ₹3.9 LPA - ₹18.0 LPA

Some companies that hire for this role are,

  • Warner Bros. Animation
  • Sony Pictures Animation
  • Nickelodeon
  • Cartoon Network
  • Ubisoft
  • Activision Blizzard
  • Electronic Arts

VFX artist

A VFX artist, or visual effects artist, is a creative professional who creates and manipulates special effects for films, television shows, video games, and other digital media. VFX artists use a variety of software and techniques to create realistic and believable effects, such as explosions, fires, and weather conditions. They also work to create more fantastical effects, such as creatures, monsters, and otherworldly environments.

VFX artists typically work in close collaboration with directors, producers, and other creative professionals to develop the visual style and effects for a project. They also need to have a strong understanding of physics, lighting, and rendering.

Salary Range: ₹3.1 LPA - ₹12.5 LPA

  • Framestore
  • MPC
  • DNEG
  • Rodeo FX
  • Rising Sun Pictures
  • Luma Pictures
  • Pixomondo

Digital Illustrator

A Digital Illustrator is a creative professional who uses digital tools to create and manipulate images. They work in a variety of industries, including advertising, publishing, video games, and web design.

Digital illustrators typically have a strong understanding of art fundamentals, such as color theory, composition, and perspective. They also need to be proficient in using digital illustration software, such as Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

Salary Range: ₹3.6 LPA - ₹12.0 LPA

  • Disney
  • Pixar
  • DreamWorks
  • Warner Bros.
  • Sony Pictures
  • BBC
  • National Geographic

Teaching and Education

An Art Teacher is a professional who teaches students about art, including its history, theory, and practice. They develop and implement lesson plans, grade student work, and provide feedback. Art teachers may also lead extracurricular activities, such as art clubs or field trips to museums.

Salary Range: ₹2 LPA - 9 LPA

Art Therapist

An art therapist is a mental health professional who uses art to help people express and explore their emotions, thoughts, and experiences. Art therapy can be used to treat a variety of conditions, including anxiety, depression, trauma, and PTSD. It can also be used to help people cope with life challenges, such as grief, illness, and divorce.

Art therapists typically work with clients in individual or group sessions. During these sessions, clients may create art using a variety of materials, such as paint, clay, and collage. The art therapist will then help the client to reflect on their artwork and explore the emotions, thoughts, and experiences that it represents.

These are some of the career options that you can opt for when you complete bachelors in fine arts. It is always suggested to get into the field and look for job opportunities that suit your personality and passion.
